# Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)

Paracetamol is a widely used analgesic (pain reliever) and antipyretic (fever
reducer). It is available over the counter and is one of the most common
medicines worldwide. Brand names include Crocin, Dolo, Calpol, and Tylenol.

## Uses
Paracetamol is used to relieve mild to moderate pain such as headache, muscle
ache, toothache, backache, and pain from osteoarthritis. It is also used to
reduce fever caused by infections such as the common cold and flu.

## Dosage
For adults, the usual dose is 500 mg to 1000 mg every 4 to 6 hours as needed.
Do not exceed 4000 mg (4 grams) in 24 hours. For children, dosing is based on
body weight (typically 10-15 mg/kg per dose). Always follow the label or a
doctor's instructions.

## Side Effects
Paracetamol is generally well tolerated at recommended doses. Rare side effects
include skin rash, allergic reactions, and low blood counts. Overdose can cause
severe and potentially fatal liver damage.

## Drug Interactions
Paracetamol may interact with warfarin, increasing bleeding risk with regular
use. Combining it with other paracetamol-containing products can lead to
accidental overdose. Carbamazepine, phenytoin, and isoniazid may increase the
risk of liver toxicity.

## Contraindications
Do not use in patients with severe liver disease or known hypersensitivity to
paracetamol. Use with caution in chronic alcohol users.

## Warnings
Exceeding the recommended dose can cause serious liver injury. Avoid alcohol
while taking paracetamol. Seek immediate medical care if an overdose is
suspected, even if you feel well.

## Pregnancy Safety
Paracetamol is generally considered the analgesic of choice during pregnancy
when used at the lowest effective dose for the shortest time. Consult a doctor
before regular use.

## Food Interactions
Paracetamol can be taken with or without food. Avoid alcohol, which increases
the risk of liver damage.

## Storage
Store below 25°C in a dry place, away from direct light and out of reach of
children.
