# Amoxicillin

Amoxicillin is a penicillin-type antibiotic used to treat a wide range of
bacterial infections. It works by stopping the growth of bacteria. Brand names
include Mox, Amoxil, and Novamox.

## Uses
Amoxicillin treats infections of the chest (pneumonia, bronchitis), ear, nose,
throat, urinary tract, and skin. It is also used as part of combination therapy
for Helicobacter pylori stomach ulcers.

## Dosage
For adults, a common dose is 250-500 mg every 8 hours, or 500-875 mg every 12
hours, depending on the infection. Complete the full prescribed course even if
symptoms improve, to prevent antibiotic resistance.

## Side Effects
Common side effects include nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, and skin rash. Serious
but rare effects include severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) and
antibiotic-associated colitis.

## Drug Interactions
Amoxicillin may reduce the effectiveness of oral contraceptives. It interacts
with allopurinol (increased rash risk), methotrexate (increased toxicity), and
probenecid (increased amoxicillin levels). It may affect warfarin control.

## Contraindications
Do not use in patients with a known allergy to penicillin or cephalosporin
antibiotics. Use with caution in patients with kidney impairment or
mononucleosis.

## Warnings
Stop the medicine and seek emergency care if signs of a severe allergic reaction
occur (rash, swelling of the face or throat, difficulty breathing). Do not use
antibiotics for viral infections such as the common cold.

## Pregnancy Safety
Amoxicillin is generally considered safe during pregnancy and breastfeeding when
prescribed by a doctor, but should only be used when clearly needed.

## Food Interactions
Can be taken with or without food. Taking it with food may reduce stomach upset.

## Storage
Store capsules and tablets below 25°C. Reconstituted oral suspension should be
kept refrigerated and discarded after 7-14 days as directed.
