# Ibuprofen

Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve pain,
reduce inflammation, and lower fever. Common brand names include Brufen, Advil,
and Nurofen.

## Uses
Ibuprofen is used for headache, dental pain, menstrual cramps, muscle and joint
pain, arthritis, and to reduce fever. Its anti-inflammatory action makes it
useful for sprains and inflammatory conditions.

## Dosage
For adults, a typical dose is 200-400 mg every 4 to 6 hours as needed, not
exceeding 1200 mg per day without medical advice. Take with food or milk to
reduce stomach upset.

## Side Effects
Common side effects include stomach pain, heartburn, nausea, and indigestion.
More serious effects include stomach ulcers, gastrointestinal bleeding, kidney
problems, and increased blood pressure with long-term use.

## Drug Interactions
Ibuprofen can interact with blood thinners such as warfarin (increased bleeding),
aspirin, other NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors and diuretics (reduced effect and kidney
risk), and lithium (increased lithium levels). Avoid combining with other NSAIDs.

## Contraindications
Do not use in patients with active stomach ulcers, severe heart failure, severe
kidney impairment, or known hypersensitivity to NSAIDs or aspirin.

## Warnings
NSAIDs may increase the risk of heart attack, stroke, and gastrointestinal
bleeding, especially with long-term use. Use the lowest effective dose for the
shortest duration.

## Pregnancy Safety
Avoid ibuprofen during the third trimester of pregnancy, as it can harm the
unborn baby and affect labour. Consult a doctor before use in pregnancy.

## Food Interactions
Take with food or milk to minimise stomach irritation. Avoid alcohol, which
increases the risk of stomach bleeding.

## Storage
Store at room temperature below 25°C, away from moisture and heat.
