# Common Drug Interactions Reference

This reference summarises selected, well-documented drug-drug interactions for
educational use. It is not exhaustive. Always confirm interactions with a
licensed pharmacist or doctor before combining medicines.

## Ibuprofen and Warfarin
Combining the NSAID ibuprofen with the anticoagulant warfarin significantly
increases the risk of gastrointestinal bleeding. This combination should be
avoided or used only under close medical supervision with monitoring.

## Paracetamol and Warfarin
Regular, high-dose paracetamol use can enhance the blood-thinning effect of
warfarin, raising bleeding risk. Occasional single doses are generally
considered low risk.

## Ibuprofen and Aspirin
Taking ibuprofen together with low-dose aspirin can reduce aspirin's
cardioprotective (heart-protecting) effect and increases the risk of stomach
bleeding. Space the doses or seek medical advice.

## Amoxicillin and Oral Contraceptives
Antibiotics such as amoxicillin may, in some cases, reduce the effectiveness of
oral contraceptive pills. Additional contraceptive precautions may be advised
during and shortly after the course.

## Multiple NSAIDs Together
Taking two or more NSAIDs (for example ibuprofen with naproxen or diclofenac)
together does not improve pain relief but substantially increases the risk of
stomach ulcers, bleeding, and kidney damage. Avoid combining NSAIDs.

## Metformin and Contrast Dye
Metformin should usually be paused around the time of imaging procedures that
use iodinated contrast dye, due to a rare risk of lactic acidosis. Follow the
radiology team's instructions.
